# <span style="color:#fabd2f"> DNA replication

## <span style="color:#fabd2f">DNA
- Two antiparallel strands --> 2 strands aligned in opposite directions
- Pairs contain 1 larger and 1 smaller base, which form complementary pairs:
  - [A]Denine-T --> 2 hydrogen bonds
  - G-C --> 3 hydrogen bonds
- Double-helical shape
- Backbone: Deoxyribose sugar


## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Steps of DNA replicaton
- Semi-conerservative process --> One strand of the original DNA goes to each new strand
###  `Steps:`
- [Helicase](#helicase) --> Separation
- [Primase](#primase) --> Priming
- [DNA polymerase](#dna-polymerase) --> Copying
- DNA Polymerase --> proofreading --> error rate: 1/10<sup>10</sup>

### <span style="color:#fabd2f">Helicase
- Breaks the hydrogen bonds between the pairs
- Unwinds the double helical strands
- SSB proteins keep the strands separated

### <span style="color:#fabd2f">Primase
- AKA: RNA polymerase
- Creates RNA `primers` used by DNA polymerase to begin work


### <span style="color:#fabd2f">DNA polymerase
- Can only work in 5' to 3' direction
- Only one strand is 5' to 3' --> New primers have to keep being placed on the `'lagging' strand`
- This creates so-called `'Okazaki fragments'`
- `DNA Ligase` connects Okazaki fragments
