# <span style="color:#fabd2f">Plant cells and organelles

# <span style="color:#fabd2f">plant cells
## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Outside
-  `rigid & rectangular`
-  Have `chloroplasts`
-  `cell wall`
   -  Provides `protection & rigidity`
   -  Made up of `cellulose`
## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Inside
- Cytoplasm
  - Contains `cell organelles`
  - Provides a `place for metabolic processes`
  - Mostly contains `water & enzymes`
## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Membranes
- Separates cell from the environment
- Semi-permeable
- Controls in-, and outflow
- Mainly made of `phospholipids` (+ proteins & carbohydrates)
  - `Carbohydrates` make membranes `specific`
  - `Proteins` form channels for polar & charged materials to enter and leave
- Mosaic-like structure
- [Transport processes](#transport-processes-across-membranes)
- Functions as a receptor site
## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Endoplasmic reticulum 
- Separates different sites of metabolic processes
  - Different metabolic processes can thus happen at the same time
  - This encreases efficiency of eukarytotic cells
### <span style="color:#fabd2f">Rough endoplasmic reticulum (RER)
- Ribosomes sit on the surface
- Site of protein production
### <span style="color:#fabd2f">Smooth endoplasmic reticulum (SER)
- Produces and stores lipids
## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Golgi body (golgi apparatus)
- System of stacked disc-shapes sacks --> Cisternae
- Purpose:
  - `Transform` proteins
  - `Distribute` proteins
## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Lysosomes
- Products of the [Golgi apparatus](#golgi-body-golgi-apparatus)
- Break down damages / old organelles inside the cell
## <span style="color:#fabd2f"> Sap vacoule
- Only in `plants`
- Can fill 90% of the cell
- Product of the [Golgi apparatus](#golgi-body-golgi-apparatus)
    - Keeps the cell `firm`
## <span style="color:#fabd2f"> Chloroplasts
- Surrounded by `double mebrane`
- Has its `own DNA`
- May be derived from ancient photoautotrophic prokaryotes --> Endosymbiosis
- Parts:
  - Thylakoid membrane: Flattened sacs within the chloroplast
  - Grana: Stacks of thylakoids
  - Stroma: Liquid surrounding the thylakoids
## <span style="color:#fabd2f"> Mitochondria
- Surrounded by `double membrane`
- Has its `own DNA`
- May also have originated through endosymbiosis
- Center of ATP production in eukaryotes & animal cells
## <span style="color:#fabd2f"> Nucleus
- Contains the `genetic information`
- `Nucleolus` --> Makes ribosomes


## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Transport processes across membranes
- `Passive:`
  - Needs no energy
  - Concentration gradient is used for transport --> Diffusion
- `Active:`
  - Needs energy
  - Transport `against` the concentration gradient
