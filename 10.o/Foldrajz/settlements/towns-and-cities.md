# <span style="color:#fabd2f">Towns and cities (=urban settlements)
- Hard to define what a city is
## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Definitions of cities
- Minimum inhabitant count (finland, sweden)
- `Minimum inhabitant` count + `number of non-agricultural workers` (India)
- Population size + Good infrastructure + "central role"

## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Location (requirements)
- Nearby food supply
- Dirnking water
- Crossroad / Junction
- Natural Defence (mountains, hills, rivers)
- Bridging point (somewhere where the river is narrow and an thus be crossed)
- Harbour
- Flood avoidance
- Building materials (e.g Kőbánya)

# <span style="color:#fabd2f">Urban revolution (historical review)
- First walled city: `Jericho` --> 9-8000 BCE
## <span style="color:#fabd2f">Chronology
### Mesopotamia:
  - 5000-3000 BCE
  - Ur, Uruk, Babylon
### Egypt:
  - Luxor, Memphis
### Hellenic Culture:
  - Appearance of city-states (Athens, Spart)
  - Self-governing military, government & economic unit
  - Some cities with 300k people
### Roman Empire
- Rome was the center of the largest empire at the time
- ~900k people --> Largest metropolis on earth at the time
- Central heating
- Sewage system
- Baths
- Aqueducts
### Constantinople
- 9-11th CE --> Largest, most wealthy city of the time
- First city to reach 1 million people
### Middle ages
- Cities were:
  - religious centres (bishopric, archbishopric)
  - educational centres (universities)
  - Commercial centres (Venice)
